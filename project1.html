Information Architecture Redesign

Role: Lead Content Designer
Timeline: 3 months
Team: Product, Design, Engineering
Tools: Figma, Miro, Analytics

Overview

Our B2B software platform's documentation was becoming increasingly difficult to navigate as our product grew. Users were spending too much time searching for information, and our support team was fielding repetitive questions about basic functionality.

The Challenge

How might we restructure our information architecture to help users find what they need quickly while supporting future content growth?

Research & Discovery

User Research

I conducted user interviews with 12 customers and analyzed 6 months of support ticket data to understand pain points:

  • Users couldn't distinguish between different types of content (tutorials vs. reference docs)
  • Search functionality returned too many irrelevant results
  • Critical information was buried 3-4 clicks deep
  • Mobile experience was particularly challenging
User Journey Map - Before State

Content Audit

I inventoried 200+ pieces of content and identified:

  • 30% duplicate or outdated content
  • Inconsistent categorization
  • Missing content for key user tasks
  • No clear content hierarchy
  • Inconsistent naming conventions
  • Poor cross-linking between related topics

Strategy & Solution

Key Design Decisions

  • Task-oriented navigation: Organized content by what users want to accomplish
  • Progressive disclosure: Surface most common information first
  • Content types: Clear visual distinction between tutorials, references, and troubleshooting
